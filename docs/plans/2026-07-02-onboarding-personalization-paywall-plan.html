Yearlit Onboarding Personalization And Paywall Plan

Decide whether to add motivation-based personalization, optional name capture, habit color selection, a founder note, and pre-paywall social proof while protecting the current activation moment: create a first habit and mark the first dot before the paywall.

Recommended: next release 2026-07-02 Planning only Medium UX risk
Recommended Decision

Ship the personalization spine, but gate it behind measurement.

Keep the existing first-dot activation before the paywall. Add motivation as the copy lens, merge color selection into habit creation, keep name optional, and include founder/social-proof screens as measured confidence builders in the next release.

Goal

Increase onboarding trust, emotional relevance, and paywall intent without delaying the first real value moment.

What Changes

Add motivation, optional name, color choice, credibility copy, founder note, and social proof before paywall.

What Stays

User still creates a first habit and marks day one before seeing the subscription ask.

Main Risk

More screens can lower onboarding completion. The plan must be tested as a funnel, not judged by taste.

Approval Needed

Approve the exact new screens, social-proof numbers, PostHog funnel events, and next-release guardrails.

Decision Matrix

Option Pros Cons Risk Decision
Keep current onboarding Lowest completion risk. Already reaches habit creation and first dot quickly. Less personalized. Paywall may still feel like a cold app asking for money. Missed conversion upside. Rejected for next release.
Add the full proposed flow without measurement Max trust-building surface. Strong narrative before paywall. No way to know which screen helped or hurt. More friction before paywall. Completion can drop. High. Rejected.
Full flow in next release with complete PostHog tracking Ships the intended human, trust-building onboarding while making every added screen measurable. Still increases screen count. Requires disciplined event coverage and fast post-release review. Medium. Recommended.

Privacy boundary

The product context says not to track user-entered sensitive content. Motivation enum is acceptable. Raw name, habit name, and custom text should not be sent to analytics.

PostHog dashboard views
  • Full onboarding funnel: step-by-step conversion from onboarding start to purchase success.
  • Drop-off by screen: onboarding_step_viewed to next-step action, broken down by step_id.
  • Time cost: median seconds_to_first_dot, seconds_to_paywall, and seconds_to_onboarding_complete by onboarding flow.
  • Motivation performance: activation, paywall view, purchase start, purchase success, D1, and D7 by onboarding_motivation.
  • Trust screen audit: conversion after why_this_works, founder_note, and social_proof; cut any screen with high exit and no downstream lift.
  • Quality of intent: compare paywall CTA rate to purchase success. If CTA rises but purchases do not, the copy is creating weak intent.
PostHog privacy rules

Allowed: motivation enum, color ID, step IDs, flow IDs, booleans, counts, timings, package type, purchase outcome. Forbidden: raw name, habit name, goal text, notification text, notes, or any other user-entered sensitive content. Add any new forbidden keys to AnalyticsCatalog.forbiddenSensitivePropertyKeys.

The reviewer should assume the author is biased toward the new idea. Their job is to prove whether the proposed flow is actually better than the current flow, not to validate the concept.

Open Questions For Approval

Should name be included in the next release?

Why it matters: Name can personalize copy, but it is also pure friction and a privacy-sensitive ask.

Recommended default: Include it only if skip is visible, the field is optional, and the raw value is never tracked.

Alternative: Remove the name screen and rely on motivation-based copy only.

Which social-proof numbers are real enough to show?

Why it matters: Weak or unverifiable stats hurt trust more than they help conversion.

Recommended default: Use App Store rating only if strong and current. Add usage stats only from reliable analytics or backend counts.

Alternative: Replace stats with product-philosophy confidence copy until numbers are stronger.

Which screens are mandatory for the next release?

Why it matters: The full proposal is the direction, but any weak screen needs a clear reason to exist before implementation.

Recommended default: Ship motivation, optional name, habit color, first-dot copy, founder note, social proof, and paywall personalization together with full PostHog tracking.

Alternative: Remove only screens that lack real copy, real data, or a measurable decision value.

What is the kill threshold?

Why it matters: Without a pre-set guardrail, a prettier flow can survive despite worse activation.

Recommended default: Kill or cut screens if first-dot activation drops more than 5% relative or onboarding completion drops more than 7% relative without a purchase lift that clearly offsets it.

Alternative: Use stricter thresholds if acquisition volume is high and you can afford fast iteration.
