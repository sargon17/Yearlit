# Product Marketing Context

## Product

Yearlit is an iOS app for calendar-based habit tracking and yearly reflection. It helps people commit to a year, track repeated action without noisy gamification, and see long-term progress through calendars, recaps, mood tracking, milestones, sharing, and widgets.

## Audience

People who want a simple, visual way to stay consistent with habits or goals across a year. They value reflection, momentum, and identity-based progress more than complex task management.

## Core Promise

Yearlit helps users become the kind of person who keeps showing up, one day or week at a time.

## Key Activation Moments

- Completing onboarding.
- Creating the first calendar.
- Completing the first check-in.
- Completing the first period.
- Returning after the first day/week.
- Enabling supporting retention features such as reminders, Mood Tracking, Recap View, and Milestone Celebrations.

## Monetization Context

Yearlit depends on users feeling enough long-term value to commit, ideally through premium/annual monetization. Paywall analytics should focus first on trigger exposure and product context, while purchase/subscription lifecycle remains handled by RevenueCat until a deeper paywall analytics pass.

## Analytics Priorities

Track data that answers product and marketing decisions:

- Where users drop from onboarding to first meaningful action.
- Which early actions predict retention and premium intent.
- Which features users enable and actually use.
- How many calendars users keep active, archive, and configure.
- Which paywall triggers users see.
- Whether sharing and recap behavior signal organic growth or long-term value.

## Privacy Boundaries

Do not track user-entered sensitive content, including calendar names, habit names, goal text, journal notes, mood note text, or notification text. Prefer booleans, counts, feature state, event names, and coarse configuration properties.
